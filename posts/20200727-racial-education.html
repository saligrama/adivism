A Brief Look at Racial Disparities in Education

Posted July 27, 2020

Previous Next

Post caption

A public school system ideally ensures equal access to education and equitable enrichment for all its students. Yet, in the United States, several systemic factors prevent the achievement of this goal.

In the distant past, school segregation was codified into Jim Crow laws. Today, a patchwork of formal and informal policies, combined with racist and classist sentiment, continues to have a similar effect on the school system. Students of color and of low-income backgrounds face a strong set of obstacles to experiencing a comparable quality of education as their white peers.

In this post, we explore the systematic flaws that still act as barriers to educational equity.
