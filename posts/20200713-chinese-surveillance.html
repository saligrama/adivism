A Brief Look at the Chinese Surveillance State

Posted July 13, 2020

Previous Next

Post caption

While mainland China has never established a democratic or free society, both in modern and historical times, the election of president Xi Jinping combined with the proliferation of technologies such as facial recognition and mass data collection have helped the country increase its grip on the lives of its citizens. In an effort to control its population and ensure a widespread deference to the Communist Party government, China has taken measures that range from restrictive, to Orwellian, to real abuses of human rights.

In this post, we take a look at each of these measures and the alarming degree to which they infringe upon the privacy and freedoms of China’s citizens.
