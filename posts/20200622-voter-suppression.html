A Brief Look at Voter Suppression

Posted June 22, 2020

Previous Next

Post caption

It's often far easier to tell someone to "just vote" than it is for that person to actually take your advice and go to the polls, particularly if they're poor or are a racial minority.

In the United States, a number of institutional factors create what are often insurmountable roadblocks to voting. Some are the result of lapses in policy judgement, but most are intentional factors designed to prevent these historically marginalized groups from participating in our democracy. Unfortunately, in some ways, we haven't progressed very far from the days of poll taxes and literacy tests in the Jim Crow era.

In this post, we explore some of the systemic issues with our electoral system that without fail depress turnout to less than 65% in every election since 1912.
