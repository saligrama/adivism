A Brief Look at the Postal Service

Posted August 17, 2020

Previous Next

Post caption

The postal service has been a mainstay of American democracy since the country’s founding. Originally, it was a way for news to circulate around to the general public in order to educate the population, adding functionality and more service over the years. While it was originally a cabinet agency, the United States Postal Service was reformed as a separate, partially private corporation in 1971.

Today, the USPS handles hundreds of millions of items of mail each day. Crucially, it processes mail-in ballots, which have become increasingly important during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Yet, the postal service is under attack by President Trump and his postmaster general, Louis DeJoy. Trump strongly believes that voting by mail disproportionately helps Democrats, a claim that is unfounded, and he has defunded the postal system as it makes further changes that slow down mail and otherwise hurt its quality of service.

In this post, we look at the various functions of the USPS and its storied history, and how it has been targeted by Trump in 2020.
