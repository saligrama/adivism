A Brief Look at Regulatory Capture (aka "Swamp Politics")

Posted August 3, 2020

Previous Next

Post caption

When President Trump took office in 2017, one of his signature campaign promises had been to “drain the [Washington] swamp.” In other words, he planned to replace career politicians serving in cabinet roles, whom he portrayed as corrupt, with outsiders that could more efficiently run these executive agencies.

Three and a half years later, Trump has not at all achieved this goal. Instead, cabinet secretaries and their subordinates have only become more corrupt. While Trump cabinet appointees have often been political outsiders, they also have frequently been previously employed by, or otherwise been representatives for, the very industries they are tasked with regulating.

This conflict-of-interest has a special term: regulatory capture. In the Trump administration, multiple cabinet agencies have deliberately loosened regulation on the industries they are in charge of, often resulting in higher costs and worse service for citizens.

In this post, we look at a number of cabinet agencies that have either failed to create effective regulation or have passed outright dangerous or malicious policies as a result of regulatory capture.
